This Privacy Policy informs you in accordance with Art. 13 GDPR about the processing of personal data when you use the app ParkAlert.

2. General Principles

ParkAlert itself does not collect any personal data and does not store any user data on its own servers. The app is fully usable offline; the core function (parking timer) works without an internet connection and without consenting to advertising.

Only the integrated advertising service Google AdMob may collect data – but only if you have given your explicit consent beforehand.

This app uses Google AdMob, an advertising service provided by Google Ireland Limited, Gordon House, Barrow Street, Dublin 4, Ireland (for users in the EU/EEA) or Google LLC, 1600 Amphitheatre Parkway, Mountain View, CA 94043, USA.

What data may be processed?

  • Android Advertising ID (AAID) / device identifiers
  • IP address (possibly truncated)
  • Device information (type, operating system, screen resolution)

Purpose of processing

The data is used to display – depending on your consent – personalised or non-personalised advertisements. Personalised ads are based on your usage profile and interests.

Legal basis

Processing for personalised advertising is carried out solely on the basis of your consent pursuant to Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR and, where applicable, § 25(1) TTDSG (access to end-device information) for users in Germany.

If you do not give consent or withdraw it, only non-personalised ads will be shown. The app remains fully functional without any consent.

Legal bases: Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR; Art. 13 GDPR (information obligation); § 25(1) TTDSG; IAB Transparency and Consent Framework (TCF) 2.2

International data transfers

Google may transfer data to the United States. Google LLC is certified under the EU–US Data Privacy Framework, which the European Commission has recognised as providing an adequate level of data protection (adequacy decision of 10 July 2023).

Retention periods

Advertising-related data is stored by Google in accordance with their Privacy Policy. We have no influence over the exact storage periods. Google typically indicates retention periods of 9 months (ad data) and 18 months (usage data).

4. Consent and Withdrawal

On first launch, a consent dialogue compliant with the IAB Transparency and Consent Framework (TCF) 2.2 is shown. You can:

  • Accept all data processing (Accept all)
  • Decline all data processing (Reject all)
  • Make granular choices for individual purposes and partners

Withdrawing consent: You can withdraw or change your consent at any time with effect for the future – in the app settings (gear icon) under "Privacy & Consent".

Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out on the basis of consent prior to withdrawal. (Art. 7(3) GDPR)

5. Your Rights under GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR): You may request information about whether and which personal data concerning you is being processed.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR): You may request the correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR): You may request the deletion of your personal data under certain conditions.
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR): You may request that processing of your data be restricted.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR): You may request a machine-readable copy of your data.
  • Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR): You may object to the processing of your data.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3) GDPR): Any consent given may be withdrawn at any time with effect for the future (see Section 4).
  • Right to lodge a complaint (Art. 77 GDPR): You have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority. In Germany, this is the competent state data protection authority (Landesdatenschutzbeauftragter). More information at: www.bfdi.bund.de
